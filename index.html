01 · The Opportunity

Habit tracking is one of the
fastest-growing app categories.
DACH is still wide open.

Apps built around challenges and habit streaks have proven their model globally — 75 Hard alone generated millions of organic posts without any paid marketing. The DACH market is consuming this content in German and English, but has very few local voices leading the conversation.

Why this category works
Challenge content is inherently shareable
People document their progress publicly. A challenge app is not a tool — it's an identity. Creators don't have to "review" HealthyHabit. They live it, they share it, and their audience wants to join. The product generates content naturally.
Why now in DACH
German-language creators in this niche are underserved
Fitness, self-improvement and discipline content is dominated by English-language creators. DACH audiences actively seek German-speaking voices for the same content — the app that wins this niche first has a significant first-mover advantage.

The September launch window is smart. Post-summer, audiences return to routines, gyms fill up, and self-improvement intent peaks. It's the second-best time of year to launch a habit app — after January. That seasonal tailwind should be built into every piece of content we brief.

04 · Content Angles

Six creative hypotheses.
One launch to find the winner.

Not all creators brief the same angle. We distribute the content across 6 directions to find which message drives installs — then paid media scales the one that wins.

A
The transformation arc — Day 1 vs. Day 30
The most proven format in the habit app category. Creator starts the challenge on camera, commits publicly, and shares a closing video after a month. Before/after content in fitness and habit-building consistently outperforms all other formats on engagement and saves.
"I started the HealthyHabit 30-day challenge. Here's what happened."
B
The accountability angle — "my followers keep me honest"
Creator uses the public nature of the challenge to stay committed. They involve their audience in the journey — asking for check-ins, sharing streaks, reacting to comments. The app becomes part of the creator-audience relationship, not just a product review.
"I told my followers I'd do this for 30 days. The app is tracking it — I can't quit now."
C
The "I tried every habit app" comparison
Speaks directly to the large audience that has already tried habit trackers and given up. Creator positions HealthyHabit as the one that finally stuck — and explains specifically why. Credible, relatable, and high-intent for an audience already in the consideration phase.
"I've deleted 6 habit apps in two years. This one's different — and here's why."
D
The morning routine integration
Morning routine content is among the highest-performing formats in the DACH self-improvement niche. Creator shows HealthyHabit as a natural part of their daily system — alongside coffee, journaling, workout. The app is positioned as a life upgrade, not a task manager.
"This is how I start every morning. The app that changed my routine."
E
The "what I wish I had at 20" angle
Targets the 25–35 DACH audience reflecting on habits they wish they'd built earlier. Emotional, shareable, and positions the app as a tool for taking control. Works especially well in short-form video with a voiceover or direct-to-camera format.
"If I had this app at 20, my life would look very different."
F
The "September reset" seasonal angle
Leverages the post-summer behaviour change moment. Many people feel January-like motivation in September after the summer — new routines, back to gym, fresh goals. This angle ties the app launch directly to a cultural moment that already exists in the audience's mindset.
"September is my real New Year. This is the app I'm starting it with."

08 · Tracking & ROI

Every download attributed.
Every creator accountable.

Trackable ROI was a specific requirement for this campaign. Here's exactly how we build the measurement architecture — using Adjust, promo codes, and per-creator reporting.

Adjust tracking links
One unique link per creator
Each creator's bio link and swipe-up points to a unique Adjust deep link. Every install via that link is attributed to that specific creator. You see installs, cost per install, and conversion rate at the individual creator level — not just campaign totals.
Promo codes
A second attribution layer — and a conversion booster
Each creator receives a personal promo code (e.g. LUKAS30, ANNA30) to share in their content. Codes serve two purposes: they create a download incentive for the audience, and they provide a second data point to cross-reference with Adjust — especially useful for Stories and TikTok where link tracking is imperfect.
Free version of Adjust
Good enough for this campaign size
Adjust's free tier supports up to 2,000 attributed installs per month — sufficient for a conservative launch scenario. If the campaign outperforms and you need more attribution capacity, upgrading mid-campaign is straightforward. We'll set up the campaigns with upgrade headroom in mind from the start.
Reporting
Weekly performance snapshots
Ramped delivers weekly performance snapshots during the campaign: installs per creator, cost per install, content engagement (views, saves, shares), and top-performing hooks. This data feeds directly into paid media targeting decisions and informs which creator profiles to scale in future campaigns.

On Adjust's free tier limit: 2,000 attributed installs/month is the current free cap. If the campaign hits this early — which is a good problem to have — the paid plan starts at ~$99/month and unlocks unlimited attribution. We recommend setting this threshold as a trigger: if organic installs pass 1,500 by day 10, upgrade immediately so no installs go untracked.
